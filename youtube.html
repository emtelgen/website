My Youtube

During the Covid pandemic in 2020 I decided to entertain myself by creating a Youtube channel that fostered my interest in military history and uniforms. This project has not only taught me the history of different militaries, but I have also learned graphic design and outputting information in an easy to understand way. I will show the software and techniques I used to create these videos further down.

Creation Procedure

Step One

Conducting research using files from different organizations such as the United States War Department and National Archives to be able to represent the information accurately.

Step Two

Using the paint.net software tool to create different section slides of the video. I export the image as multiple layers that I can manipulate in the following step to add visual elements that appeal to the viewer. I also create the thumbnail in this software.

Step Three

Writing and editing the script audio that will be used in the specific video. I use the program Audacity to record my voice which I can export as an MP3 file to my video editing software.

Step Four

Utilizing the video editing software DaVinci Resolve to add my image layers and audio script with background music to a timeline. This is where I manipulate the opacity, positioning, and size of the image layers to create a video rather than a slideshow.

Additional Modifications

Things that could improve my youtube videos would include working on the delivery of my audio with my voice intonation as well as inflections. As I improve using DaVinci Resolve I also learn new techniques that I add to make my videos even more interesting, so there are always improvements to be made in that category.
